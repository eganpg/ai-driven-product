## The Hidden Risk in How Most Companies Use AI

Right now, employees across your business are using AI tools to do their jobs better. They're writing prompts that work, building mental models of how to get good outputs, and refining processes over time. That's the value.

But in most organisations, all of that value is being stored in one place: inside the AI platform itself. And that creates three compounding risks.

| RISK 1 | RISK 2 | RISK 3 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Vendor Lock-In** | **Zero Portability** | **Competitive Erosion** |
| Your refined prompts, processes and workflows exist only inside a platform you pay to access. Change platforms, lose the work. | When a better tool emerges — and it will — you cannot migrate your institutional knowledge. You start from zero. | Competitors who own their AI workflows compound their advantage over time. Yours resets every time you change tools or lose a team member. |

## Think of It Like This

AI platforms are the land. Your skills, workflows and process knowledge are the building. Right now, most businesses are constructing valuable buildings on land they don't own.

**Building on rented land** — You construct something valuable. But the landowner can change the rent, change the rules, or sell the land. Your building is stranded.

**Building on land you own** — You construct something valuable. You control it. You can expand it, move it, or hand it to someone else — on your terms.

## What It Means to Own Your AI Workflows

Ownership is straightforward in practice. It means your processes, prompts, and workflow instructions are stored in files your business controls — not inside any AI platform.

In concrete terms:

- Reusable process instructions ('skills') are written as simple text files and stored in your own systems or repositories
- When a team member refines a workflow, that improvement is captured in the file — not lost when the session ends
- New team members inherit the full accumulated knowledge immediately, not after months of trial and error
- If you switch AI platforms, your workflows move with you — you're not starting over
- Leadership has visibility and governance over how AI is being used operationally

## The Strategic Opportunity

Every repeatable process your team currently does manually is a candidate to become a business-owned AI workflow.

Unlike traditional software, these workflows are written in plain language. No code. No expensive development cycles. Your operational experts can build and refine them directly.

## Recommended Actions

| # | Action | Owner |
| --- | --- | --- |
| *1* | Establish a company principle: all AI process workflows must be stored in business-owned files, not vendor platforms | *Leadership* |
| *2* | Identify 3–5 high-value repeatable processes to pilot as owned AI workflows — start with delivery, ops, or client work | *Ops* |
| *3* | Designate an AI Workflow Owner responsible for building, maintaining and sharing the workflow library | *Leadership* |
| *4* | Audit current AI tool usage — understand what knowledge already exists and where it currently lives | *Ops* |
| *5* | Build a simple shared repository where owned workflows are stored, versioned and accessible to the whole team | *Ops* |

The companies winning with AI are not just using it more. They are building institutional knowledge that compounds. That only happens when the business owns the workflows — not the platform.
